[[Hellenistic Philosophy - James Fieser (articles)]]

**Hellenistic philosophy** is [Ancient Greek philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek_philosophy "Ancient Greek philosophy") corresponding to the [Hellenistic period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenistic_period "Hellenistic period") in [Ancient Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece), from the death of [Alexander the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great "Alexander the Great") in [323 BC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/323_BC "323 BC") to the [Battle of Actium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Actium "Battle of Actium") in [31 BC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/31_BC "31 BC").[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenistic_philosophy#cite_note-FOOTNOTELong19961-1) The dominant schools of this period were the [Stoics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoics "Stoics"), the [Epicureans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epicureans "Epicureans") and the [Skeptics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academic_Skepticism "Academic Skepticism").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenistic_philosophy#cite_note-FOOTNOTELong1996vii-2)
